Laughter Studies
This series of pieces is based around performers verbally describing and vocally imitating assembled collages of sounds. In some versions, instruments are added. The series' name comes from the idea of a world where humans have lost the ability to do things which once came naturally to them, such as laughing. In such a world, people would have to learn how to laugh, cry, acquire language, even breathe, solely through artificially imitating pre-recorded sounds of these basic functions. (A bit like at the end of the film Synecdoche, New York, where Philip Seymour Hoffman's character hears instructions delivered through an earpiece telling him to "breathe in, breathe out, breathe in, breathe out...").
